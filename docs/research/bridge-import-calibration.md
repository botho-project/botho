# Calibrating the bridge-import epoch length `K` and floor `F`

**Issue:** [#937](https://github.com/botho-project/botho/issues/937) (blocks acceptance of [ADR 0007](../decisions/0007-bridge-import-cluster-tagging.md); unblocks implementation [#938](https://github.com/botho-project/botho/issues/938))
**Status:** Simulation + recommendation. The two constants are ratified by the maintainer at review; on ratification ADR 0007 flips **Proposed → Accepted** and #938 is unblocked.
**Reproduce:**
```
cargo run -p bth-cluster-tax --features cli --bin cluster-tax-sim -- bridge-import-sweep
```
Sim source: `cluster-tax/src/simulation/bridge_import_sweep.rs` (deterministic — a pure function of its inputs, no RNG). Every number below is regenerated by that command — nothing is hand-computed. It reuses the shipped `ClusterFactorCurve`, the shipped `TagVector::mix` value-weighted blend, the shipped `demurrage_charge` kernel, and the shared `simulation::metrics::calculate_gini` — no reimplementations.

---

## 1. What the two parameters are

ADR 0007 makes an **unwrap** (wBTH → BTH) mint into a **block-epoch import cluster**. Every unwrap in height range `[mK, (m+1)K)` joins one shared origin `c_import(m) = H("bridge-import" ‖ m)`, where `m = ⌊height / K⌋`. The cluster's wealth (the curve input) is the **sum of all unwrap amounts in the epoch**, and the production `ClusterFactorCurve` (log-sigmoid, `W_MID = 100k` BTH, saturating 6×) maps it to a factor — the identical curve domestic clusters use — clamped to a floor `F`:

```
import_factor(m) = max(F, ClusterFactorCurve(Σ unwrap amounts in epoch m))
```

Two constants block acceptance:

1. **Epoch length `K`** (blocks). Trades **split-game cost** against **co-location collateral**. A whale cannot dilute inside one epoch — intra-epoch splitting piles into the same accumulating pool and still hits the high factor. Diluting requires spreading across *epochs*, and each epoch costs `K` blocks × 5 s of wall-clock time. Short `K` → cheaper to split-game over time but little collateral; long `K` → costlier to game but a small entrant is likelier caught in a co-occurring whale flood. Candidate range (ADR §Open calibration): ~10k blocks (~14 h) to ~1 week (~120,960 blocks).
2. **Import-factor floor `F`**. Bounds the split-game payoff (the best factor a whale reaches by full dilution is `F`, not 1×) and sets the minimum bridge-entry toll. `F = 1` = no floor (split-gameable toward 1×); higher `F` penalizes *all* imports more bluntly. Candidate range (ADR §Open calibration): 1.5×–2×.

### The load-bearing structural fact

**Time-as-cost replaces provenance-as-cost.** A size-based per-unwrap factor is Sybil-able by drip-split (each dust unwrap a separate low-wealth origin at ~1×). The epoch key defeats that because all unwraps in a window share one accumulating cluster. The only way down is to spread across epochs — and the sim measures exactly how much *time* that costs, per `K`.

## 2. Epoch length `K` — split-game cost vs collateral

### 2.1 Split-game cost (at the recommended floor F = 1.5×)

A whale that dumps its whole import in one epoch pays the *undiluted* factor (the real curve at the full volume). To reach the floor it must spread the import across enough epochs that each epoch's share prices out at the floor (`epochs to floor`), and each epoch costs `K` blocks × 5 s.

| K | whale import (BTH) | undiluted factor | epochs to floor | split-game cost |
|---|-------------------:|-----------------:|----------------:|----------------:|
| 14h (14.0h) | 500000 | 4.617x | 136 | 79.3 days |
| 1d (24.0h) | 500000 | 4.617x | 136 | 136.0 days |
| 2d (48.0h) | 500000 | 4.617x | 136 | 272.0 days |
| 3.5d (84.0h) | 500000 | 4.617x | 136 | 476.0 days |
| 1wk (168.0h) | 500000 | 4.617x | 136 | 952.0 days |
| 14h (14.0h) | 2000000 | 5.448x | 541 | 315.6 days |
| 1d (24.0h) | 2000000 | 5.448x | 541 | 541.0 days |
| 2d (48.0h) | 2000000 | 5.448x | 541 | 1082.0 days |
| 3.5d (84.0h) | 2000000 | 5.448x | 541 | 1893.5 days |
| 1wk (168.0h) | 2000000 | 5.448x | 541 | 3787.0 days |
| 14h (14.0h) | 10000000 | 5.745x | 2705 | 1577.9 days |
| 1d (24.0h) | 10000000 | 5.745x | 2705 | 2705.0 days |
| 2d (48.0h) | 10000000 | 5.745x | 2705 | 5410.0 days |
| 3.5d (84.0h) | 10000000 | 5.745x | 2705 | 9467.5 days |
| 1wk (168.0h) | 10000000 | 5.745x | 2705 | 18935.0 days |

`epochs to floor` is **`K`-independent** — it is a property of the curve and the floor (how finely the import must be sliced so each slice prices at 1.5×), not of the epoch length. `K` multiplies that fixed slice count into wall-clock time. The result: even the shortest candidate epoch (14 h) forces a 2M-BTH whale to spend **≈ 316 days** dripping its import through 541 separate epochs to escape the tax, versus paying **5.448×** if it unwraps in one shot. A mega-whale (10M BTH) pays **≈ 4.3 years** at 14 h epochs. The split game is economically dead at every candidate `K`.

### 2.2 Innocent-small-entrant collateral (at F = 1.5×)

A genuine small entrant unwraps 1,000 BTH. Alone it prices at the floor 1.5×. But if a whale flood lands in the *same* epoch, the entrant inherits the shared pool's factor `curve(small + flood)`:

| flood regime | flood volume (BTH) | entrant alone | entrant caught | collateral ratio |
|--------------|-------------------:|--------------:|---------------:|-----------------:|
| low | 5000 | 1.500x | 1.588x | 1.059x |
| medium | 100000 | 1.500x | 3.505x | 2.337x |
| high | 2000000 | 1.500x | 5.448x | 3.632x |

`K` does not change the *severity* when caught (the shared pool is per-epoch regardless of `K`) — it changes the *probability* of co-occurrence. A longer epoch is a wider net: the longer the window, the more likely a small honest entrant shares it with a flood. This is the ADR's explicit shared-fate coupling ("you cannot have all three of {Sybil-resistant, no-shared-fate, no-external-identity}").

Two facts blunt the collateral even when it lands:

- **The floor is the same in every non-flood epoch.** An entrant who avoids a flood epoch pays exactly 1.5× — the toll they were always going to pay. The collateral is a *transient* over-charge, not a permanent classification.
- **It blends off in ~9 spends (§4).** A small entrant caught at 5.448× normalizes to the floor after roughly nine ordinary domestic spends — days-to-weeks of normal use — because its own coin is tiny relative to the domestic money it mixes with.

### 2.3 `K` recommendation

The split game is dead at every candidate `K` (§2.1), so **`K` should be chosen to minimize collateral exposure** — i.e. the *shortest* window that still amortizes bridge/consensus bookkeeping sensibly. The 14 h and 1-day epochs both leave the split game hopeless (316 / 541 days for a 2M whale) while giving the *narrowest* co-occurrence net. We recommend **`K` = 1 day (17,280 blocks at 5 s)**: a round operational unit (one import cluster per calendar day is trivially auditable and human-legible), a 541-day split-game wall for a 2M-BTH whale, and a co-occurrence window short enough that a small entrant can simply unwrap on a quiet day. 14 h is a defensible alternative if minimizing collateral is weighted above operational tidiness; we do not recommend going longer than 1 day — 1-week epochs buy nothing on the split game (already dead) and only widen the collateral net (7× the co-occurrence exposure of a daily epoch).

## 3. Import-factor floor `F` — residual anti-hoarding vs onboarding friction

For each candidate `F`: the effective factor a genuine small entrant (1,000 BTH) pays (`max(F, curve(small))`); the money-terms toll (one year of demurrage at that factor, % of value); the floor a fully split-gamed whale bottoms out at; and `residual above background` (how far `F` sits above 1×, normalized to the 6×−1× span — 0 = indistinguishable from background, the entry leak; 1 = maximally taxed).

| F | small-entrant factor | annual toll (% of value) | gamed-whale floor | residual above background |
|---|---------------------:|-------------------------:|------------------:|--------------------------:|
| 1.000x | 1.265x | 0.1060% | 1.000x | 0.000 |
| 1.250x | 1.265x | 0.1060% | 1.250x | 0.050 |
| 1.500x | 1.500x | 0.2000% | 1.500x | 0.100 |
| 1.750x | 1.750x | 0.3000% | 1.750x | 0.150 |
| 2.000x | 2.000x | 0.4000% | 2.000x | 0.200 |

Three findings the data forces:

1. **The floor is what survives full split-gaming.** `gamed-whale floor` = `F` in every row. With no floor (`F = 1.0×`) a whale that drips across 136+ epochs reaches ~1×: the entire anti-hoarding premium is gameable to nothing, given enough patience. The floor is the *residual* premium the split game cannot erode — it is the whole point of having a floor.
2. **A retail import is not at 1× on the raw curve.** A 1,000-BTH import already prices at **1.265×** on the production curve (finding worth flagging: "small" on the log-domain curve still means thousands of BTH). So `F` below ~1.27× is invisible to a small entrant — the floor only *binds* (and only starts taxing) once it exceeds the coin's natural curve factor. This is why the `F = 1.0×` and `F = 1.25×` rows show the same small-entrant factor and toll.
3. **The onboarding friction is genuinely mild in money terms.** Even at `F = 2.0×` the annual demurrage toll on a small entrant is **0.40 % of value per year** — and that toll only applies *until the coin circulates* (§4), after which it blends toward background. This is the "minimum toll for entering via the bridge rather than earning domestically" the ADR intends, and it is nowhere near a liquidity-breaking level.

### 3.1 `F` recommendation

`F = 1.0×` is rejected: it makes the whole premium split-gameable to nothing (finding 1) — the mechanism would exist but be bypassable by any patient whale, exactly the defect ADR 0007 exists to close. Among the real floors, the trade is linear (each +0.25× buys +0.05 residual for +0.10 %/yr toll), so the choice is a judgment call on how much residual anti-hoarding is worth. We recommend **`F` = 1.5×**:

- It sits at residual **0.100** — a clear, non-trivial premium above background that a split-gaming whale cannot erode, yet well short of the blunt 2× that taxes every honest entrant at 0.40 %/yr.
- The onboarding toll is **0.20 %/yr** — negligible against DeFi-liquidity motives, and transient (gone after ~9 spends).
- It is the low end of the ADR's own 1.5×–2× candidate window, biasing toward onboarding-friendliness in the acknowledged tension. If the maintainer weights anti-hoarding above onboarding, 1.75× is the next step up (residual 0.150, toll 0.30 %/yr) with the same qualitative behavior; 2× is defensible only if the bridge is viewed primarily as a concentration vector to be discouraged.

## 4. Decay by circulation (the third experiment)

An imported coin's factor falls **only** by circulating. Using the real `TagVector::mix` value-weighted blend, a worst-case flood import that entered at **6×** and receives one payment of its own value in domestic (background) money per spend normalizes as follows:

| domestic-mixing spends | import weight | import weight (frac) | effective factor |
|-----------------------:|-------------:|---------------------:|-----------------:|
| 0 | 1000000 | 1.0000 | 6.000x |
| 1 | 500000 | 0.5000 | 3.500x |
| 2 | 333333 | 0.3333 | 2.666x |
| 3 | 249999 | 0.2500 | 2.249x |
| 4 | 199999 | 0.2000 | 1.999x |
| 5 | 166665 | 0.1667 | 1.833x |
| 6 | 142855 | 0.1429 | 1.714x |
| 7 | 124998 | 0.1250 | 1.624x |
| 8 | 111109 | 0.1111 | 1.555x |
| 9 | 99998 | 0.1000 | 1.499x |
| 10 | 90907 | 0.0909 | 1.454x |
| 15 | 62496 | 0.0625 | 1.312x |
| 20 | 47614 | 0.0476 | 1.238x |

*(Table abridged for the doc; the command prints every spend 0–20.)*

**Finding:** a worst-case 6× flood import blends down to the recommended **1.5× floor in ≈ 9 domestic spends** — days-to-weeks of ordinary use. The decay is the harmonic `1/(s+1)` shape of value-weighted mixing (weight halves on the first equal-value spend, then thirds, quarters…), so the *early* spends do most of the normalization: after just 4 spends the worst-case import is already under 2×. Imported wealth that actually enters the domestic economy stops being "expensive" quickly; only wealth that *sits at the boundary* keeps paying.

**The intended invariant holds:** a pure-external holder who **never receives domestic money** (50 self-spends, zero domestic inflow) keeps effective factor **6.000×** — unchanged. The real blend leaves a never-mixing coin untouched (mixing with zero incoming value is a no-op on the tag). There is no time-based decay: sitting idle, or churning only among other external-only holders, never reaches background. This is ADR 0007 §4 exactly — *using* imported wealth in the domestic economy is what normalizes it; hoarding it at the boundary does not.

## 5. Concentration signal (why the shared-fate coupling is a feature)

Gini of the population's effective-spendable wealth (value ÷ factor — how much *low-cost purchasing power* each holder commands) for 100 domestic factor-1 holders (1,000 BTH each) + one 2,000,000-BTH bridge flood:

| flood enters at | Gini (effective-spendable) |
|-----------------|---------------------------:|
| factor-1 (ADR 0003 status quo) | 0.9425 |
| floor F (ADR 0007) | 0.7760 |

Under ADR 0003 the flood enters at 1× and **dominates the cheap-money distribution** (Gini 0.94 — one holder commands almost all the spendable purchasing power). Under ADR 0007 it enters at its epoch factor (5.448× here), which divides its spendable footprint by that factor; the effective-spendable Gini falls to 0.78. The gap (0.94 → 0.78) is the **entry leak ADR 0007 narrows**: a sudden capital flood — the inflow that would most dilute the domestic distribution — is priced as maximally concentrated, exactly as the ADR intends ("bridge-inflow-*rate* becomes a concentration signal"). An organic trickle (the "low" regime, §2.2) enters near the floor and barely moves the distribution.

## 6. Recommendation

| Parameter | Recommended value | Blocks (5 s) | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------:|-----------|
| Epoch length `K` | **1 day** | 17,280 | Split game already dead at every candidate `K` (a 2M-BTH whale needs 541 days to dilute to the floor); so choose the shortest operationally-tidy window to minimize collateral exposure. One import cluster per calendar day is human-legible and trivially auditable. |
| Import-factor floor `F` | **1.5×** | (factor units) | The residual anti-hoarding a split-gaming whale cannot erode (residual 0.100), at an onboarding toll of 0.20 %/yr — transient, gone in ~9 spends. `F = 1.0×` is rejected (whole premium gameable to nothing); 1.75× / 2× are the anti-hoarding-weighted alternatives. |

**Trade at the recommendation.** At `K` = 1 day / `F` = 1.5×: a 2M-BTH whale must burn **541 days** to split-game its import down to 1.5× (vs paying 5.448× in one shot) — the split game is economically dead. The price is that a small honest entrant who happens to unwrap in the *same* daily epoch as a 2M-BTH flood is transiently caught at up to **5.448×** (3.6× collateral) — but that over-charge blends back to the 1.5× floor in ~9 ordinary spends, and a one-day window is a narrow net to have to avoid. We judge the split-game death worth the transient, self-healing collateral, in line with the ADR's stance that shared fate is the intrinsic price of identity-free Sybil-resistance.

**Decay finding.** A worst-case 6× flood import normalizes to the 1.5× floor in ≈ 9 domestic spends (the value-weighted blend does most of the work in the first 4), while a pure-external holder that never receives domestic money stays at 6× forever — the intended ADR 0007 §4 behavior, confirmed against the real `TagVector::mix` kernel.

**Sensitivity.** The `K` conclusion is robust across the whole candidate range — the split game is dead from 14 h to 1 week, so the only lever `K` moves is collateral *probability*, which monotonically favors shorter epochs. The `F` conclusion is a linear trade (each +0.25× buys +0.05 residual for +0.10 %/yr toll) with no cliff, so the maintainer can slide it toward anti-hoarding (1.75×/2×) without changing any qualitative behavior; the sim regenerates the exact numbers for any choice.

**The operator ratifies the final values.** On ratification, ADR 0007 flips Proposed → Accepted and #938 (implementation) is unblocked.
